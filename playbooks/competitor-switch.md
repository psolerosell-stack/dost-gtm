# Playbook: Competitor Switch

*How to handle accounts that are currently using a competitor — whether they're evaluating alternatives or locked in.*

---

## Trigger

One of the following:
- Signal fires on an account that uses a competitor (technographic)
- Prospect mentions a competitor in a reply or discovery call
- G2 / review site intent signal from a competitor's review page
- Inbound from an account that came from a competitor's market

---

## Step 1: Identify the Competitive Situation

Not all competitive situations are the same. Determine which scenario applies:

**Scenario A: They use a competitor, haven't heard of us**
They're not actively looking, but there's a technographic fit signal. Standard outreach with competitive angle.

**Scenario B: Active evaluation — comparing us to a competitor**
They've reached out or replied. They're running an evaluation. Time-sensitive.

**Scenario C: Recently reviewed a competitor negatively**
Intent signal from G2 or a review platform. High intent, specific pain identified.

**Scenario D: Currently under contract with a competitor**
Not actively evaluating. Play a long game — stay visible, let them come to you when renewal approaches.

---

## Scenario A: They Use a Competitor, Haven't Heard of Us

Reference `context/competitor-radar.md` for the relevant battlecard.

**Outreach angle:** Lead with insight about what companies at their stage typically find limiting about [Competitor]. Don't name the competitor directly in the first touch.

```
Read context/competitor-radar.md and skills/account-research/SKILL.md.
Research [company.com]. They use [Competitor] based on technographic data.
Build a first touch for [persona] that uses the competitive angle from the
battlecard without naming the competitor directly. The hook should reference
a specific pain that [Competitor] users at their scale commonly hit.
```

**What not to do:** Don't say "I saw you use [Competitor] and I think we're better." It's combative and it rarely lands. Lead with the insight, not the comparison.

---

## Scenario B: Active Evaluation — Comparing to Competitor

Time is short. Do this within 24 hours of learning about the evaluation.

**Step 1: Understand what they care about**
Review the conversation history and any notes from the first call. What dimension of the evaluation matters most to them?

**Step 2: Pull the relevant battlecard**
```
Read context/competitor-radar.md for the [Competitor] battlecard.
What are the 2-3 most relevant differentiators for an account that cares
most about [dimension from conversation]?
```

**Step 3: Prepare the comparison**
Build a concise side-by-side that focuses on their stated criteria. Don't build a generic feature matrix — build a targeted comparison that addresses their specific decision factors.

**Step 4: Equip your champion**
Your champion is selling internally. Give them language:
- What to say when asked about [Competitor]
- Why [your product] is the right choice for where they're going, not just where they are now
- A reference customer story that mirrors their situation

---

## Scenario C: Negative Competitor Review (G2 Intent)

This is the highest-intent competitive signal. Act within 24 hours.

```
A prospect at [company] left a [2-3 star] review of [Competitor] mentioning
[pain point from review]. Write a first touch email that:
1. References the pain they described (without revealing you've read their review)
2. Connects that pain to what we solve
3. Keeps it short — this is a warm signal, not a cold pitch

Reference context/competitor-radar.md for our angle vs. [Competitor].
```

---

## Scenario D: Under Contract with Competitor

Don't push for a meeting. Plant a flag.

**Goal:** Be memorable when renewal comes up. Get one positive interaction logged.

Approach:
- Send something genuinely useful — an industry insight, a relevant framework, something they'd share with their team
- No pitch, no meeting ask
- Set a task to follow up 60 days before typical renewal (if known)

```
[Contact] is under contract with [Competitor] until approximately [date].
Write a short note that provides genuine value around [relevant topic to their role]
with no CTA beyond continuing the conversation. Reference context/profile.md
for our positioning. No pitch. No meeting ask.
```

---

## Competitive Intelligence — Keep the Battlecards Current

After every competitive win or loss, update `context/competitor-radar.md`:

- **Win:** What was the deciding factor? What did the champion say closed it?
- **Loss:** What did [Competitor] offer that we couldn't match? Was it price, features, relationships, or timing?
- **Pattern:** After 5+ wins/losses against the same competitor, summarize the pattern

The battlecards are only useful if they reflect current reality. A battlecard based on 2022 deals is misleading in 2025.
