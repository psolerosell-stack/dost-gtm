## Ideal Customer Profile

### Firmographics

| Dimension | Ideal Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Employee count | 150–2,000 | Below 150: too small to justify; above 2,000: different sales motion |
| Revenue (if known) | €10M–€300M ARR | [inferred from customer base] |
| Funding stage | Profitable / Seed–Series B | Series A+ correlates with digitization budget unlocked |
| Industry | Food, retail, manufacturing, logistics, automotive, construction, healthcare | Verticals with high supplier invoice volume and physical goods flow |
| Geography | Spain (primary), UK (expansion) | EU e-invoicing regulations add urgency in Spain; UK launch 2025 |
| Business model | B2B, physical goods / services with supplier relationships | Not pure SaaS — needs real invoice volume |

### Technographics

**Strong fit indicators:**
- Uses SAP, NetSuite, Microsoft Dynamics, or Sage — signals ERP maturity and invoice volume scale
- Uses Excel or email-based approval workflows — signals manual AP pain we directly replace
- Post-ERP implementation (go-live in last 12 months) — evaluation window for adjacent automation is open
- Hiring Finance Manager or AP/AR specialist — confirms manual workload is at capacity

**Weak fit indicators:**
- Already uses Basware or Medius as primary AP tool — switching cost high
- No dedicated finance team or <2 people in accounting — too small to need automation

### Organizational signals

- Has a Finance Manager, Head of Accounting, or Director of Finance (not just a bookkeeper)
- CFO or Finance Director is a named executive — signals budget authority in seat
- Finance team of 3+ people — enough volume to justify automation investment
- Actively hiring for AP or treasury roles — confirms invoice volume is outpacing current capacity

## Tier Definitions

### Tier 1 — Dream Accounts (Top 150)

Mid-market companies in Spain or UK with 300–1,500 employees, in food / retail / manufacturing / logistics / automotive / construction, running SAP or NetSuite, finance team of 5+ handling 1,000+ invoices per month. ERP recently implemented or upgraded. Multi-entity or multi-location structure adds urgency.

**Criteria:**
- 300–1,500 employees in Spain or UK
- Industry: food production, retail chains, manufacturing, logistics, construction, automotive
- Uses SAP, NetSuite, or Microsoft Dynamics
- Finance team of 5+ (visible on LinkedIn)
- Recent ERP go-live, funding round, or hiring surge in finance roles

**Outreach approach:** Manual, deeply researched, personalized per stakeholder. Executive-led intro where possible. Reference customer from same vertical.

---

### Tier 2 — High-Fit Accounts (500–1,000)

Same geographies, 150–300 or 1,500–3,000 employees, adjacent verticals (healthcare, professional services, energy). Has ERP but may be Sage or smaller. Finance team of 2–4. Signal-triggered.

**Criteria:**
- 150–3,000 employees in Spain or UK
- Industry: healthcare, energy, professional services, or adjacent physical goods
- Uses any ERP or accounting software (Sage, Holded, etc.)
- At least one Tier 1 or Tier 2 signal fired in last 60 days

**Outreach approach:** Signal-triggered sequences, semi-personalized by vertical and signal type.

---

### Tier 3 — Good-Fit Accounts (1,000–5,000)

Meets minimum ICP criteria but no hot signal. Automated sequence with light personalization.

**Criteria:**
- 100–3,000 employees in Spain or UK
- B2B company with supplier relationships and physical goods
- Finance team exists (at least 1 named person on LinkedIn)

**Outreach approach:** Automated sequences, vertical-specific templates.

---

### Tier 4 — Monitor Only

Too small (<100 employees), pure SaaS, public sector, or outside Spain/UK. Stay on radar if vertical fit is strong.

## Anti-ICP

| Exclusion | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Pure SaaS / software companies | Low invoice volume — AP automation is not a pain point; budget better spent elsewhere |
| <100 employees (unless exceptional volume) | No dedicated finance team, no budget, long time-to-value |
| Enterprise >3,000 employees without a named champion | Different procurement process, longer cycle, often already locked into Basware/Medius |
| Public sector / government | Procurement timelines measured in years; compliance requirements diverge |
| Companies with no ERP or accounting software | Not ready — need foundational tools first |
| Professional employer organizations (PEOs) and outsourced AP providers | Channel conflict risk; they compete with or replace us |

## Qualification Framework

### Must-have (deal-breaker if absent)
1. Finance team exists with at least one person owning AP/AR
2. Company processes invoices from external suppliers or issues invoices to clients at volume
3. Spain or UK geography (current go-to-market focus)

### Strong indicators (2+ = high confidence)
1. Uses SAP, NetSuite, Microsoft Dynamics, or Sage
2. 200+ employees with physical goods / B2B supplier relationships
3. Finance team of 3+ visible on LinkedIn
4. Recent ERP implementation, funding round, or expansion announcement
5. Hiring for accounting or treasury roles

### Red flags (2+ = deprioritize)
1. Already has a dedicated AP automation vendor (Basware, Medius, Yooz)
2. Finance team is <2 people — not enough volume to justify
3. Public sector buyer or government-funded entity
4. Primary business is professional services with low physical invoice volume
