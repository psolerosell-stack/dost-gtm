# Persona: Finance Manager / Head of Accounting

---

## Overview

**Title(s):** Finance Manager, Head of Accounting, Head of Accounting & Treasury, AP Manager, Financial Controller
**Typical seniority:** Manager / Director
**Decision role:** Champion — daily user of the product, drives internal buy and often initiates vendor evaluation
**Found at companies:** Mid-market companies with 150–2,000 employees, physical goods / B2B, Spain or UK

---

## What They Care About

**Their primary metric:** Invoice processing time, error rate, month-end close speed, team overtime during close

**Their biggest problem right now:** Volume has grown, headcount hasn't. They're spending 60–80% of their team's time on manual data entry, chasing approvals, and fixing matching errors. Every month-end is a sprint. They know it's broken but haven't had time to fix it — or the budget conversation kept getting deferred.

**What a good week looks like for them:** Invoices flow through without intervention. The CFO asks no questions because the data is already in the ERP. Month-end closes in 2 days instead of 5. The team isn't doing overtime.

**What a bad week looks like:** Supplier calls about unpaid invoices. A delivery note doesn't match the invoice, and the team spends two days tracing it. The CFO asks for an AP report and it takes half a day to compile.

---

## How They Buy

**Involvement in purchase:** Initiates the evaluation, drives the internal case, often the primary demo contact. CFO or Finance Director approves budget.
**Discovery behavior:** LinkedIn, peer recommendations, Google searches during month-end pain. Sometimes found via their ERP provider's marketplace or recommended by their accountant.
**Evaluation style:** Demo-driven — they need to see their own document formats working in the product. Will ask about ERP integration depth and onboarding timeline. References from same industry carry high weight.
**Common objections:**
- "We've tried to automate before and it didn't stick" → Show implementation timeline and first-week results; offer to speak with a reference customer
- "Our suppliers send invoices in 15 different formats" → This is exactly why proprietary AI matters; walk through format detection in demo
- "We don't have budget right now" → ROI framing: 80% cost reduction, typically pays back in <6 months; offer to help build the internal case

---

## How to Reach Them

**Best channel:** Email (direct), LinkedIn (especially if they post about finance ops)
**Best time:** Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11am; avoid last week of month (month-end close)
**What gets their attention:** Specific numbers ("80% cost reduction," "9 days to 3 days"), references from their own industry, a clear understanding of their pain without requiring them to explain it
**What gets ignored:** Generic "AI-powered automation" cold emails, anything that sounds like a sales pitch without understanding their specific situation
