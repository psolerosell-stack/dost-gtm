# Persona: CFO / Finance Director

---

## Overview

**Title(s):** CFO, Chief Financial Officer, Finance Director, VP Finance, Director of Finance
**Typical seniority:** C-Suite / VP
**Decision role:** Economic buyer — controls budget, signs the contract, final decision authority
**Found at companies:** Mid-market companies 150–2,000 employees with a dedicated finance function. Often the most senior finance person at smaller companies (150–500 employees).

---

## What They Care About

**Their primary metric:** Cost per invoice processed, cash flow cycle time, close speed, error rate impacting financials, team efficiency ratio

**Their biggest problem right now:** Finance operations cost is growing with headcount, not with technology. The board wants operational leverage. They've approved headcount for AP/AR but know it's a stop-gap. They're accountable for financial accuracy — every processing error is their error. Post-funding CFOs are under investor pressure to show a clear path to operational efficiency.

**What a good week looks like for them:** Cash flow is visible in real time. Month-end closes without heroics. The finance team is focused on analysis, not data entry. No surprise payment errors.

**What a bad week looks like:** A duplicate payment surfaces. Month-end takes 7 days because invoice reconciliation is manual. Board asks for AP aging and it takes two days to produce. A supplier escalates over a delayed payment.

---

## How They Buy

**Involvement in purchase:** Final budget approval and contract signature. Often not the initiating buyer — their Finance Manager brings the recommendation. But they can also initiate if they've experienced the pain directly or received peer pressure to automate.
**Discovery behavior:** Peer CFO networks, board recommendations, finance conferences (CFO summits, FP&A events), occasionally LinkedIn. Trust peer references above all else.
**Evaluation style:** ROI-driven. Needs to see cost reduction as a concrete number, not a percentage. Wants implementation risk minimized — ask about go-live timeline and what happens if it fails. Social proof from companies of comparable size matters.
**Common objections:**
- "What's the ROI and how fast?" → 80% cost reduction in admin; 2.5x productivity; typical payback <6 months; offer to model their specific invoice volume
- "What's the implementation risk?" → Live in days not months; no process redesign required; reference IASO: "we just had to improve them, not change them"
- "We already have [ERP] — can't it do this?" → ERPs have basic invoice capture; Dost handles the AI extraction, matching, approval routing, and supplier portal that ERPs don't

---

## How to Reach Them

**Best channel:** Peer introductions (highest conversion), LinkedIn (for thought leadership angles), email (if personalized to a specific business event)
**Best time:** Monday morning or Friday afternoon for strategic thinking; avoid earnings/close periods
**What gets their attention:** Concrete ROI numbers, peer references at same company size and industry, a specific insight about their business that shows you've done homework
**What gets ignored:** Generic "reduce costs with AI" messaging, feature lists, anything that reads like a sales email
