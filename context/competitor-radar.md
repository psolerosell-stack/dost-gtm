## Yooz

**One-line summary:** European mid-market AP automation platform with strong ERP integrations and a broad supplier network.
**Market position:** Challenger (strong in France and Southern Europe)
**Pricing:** [inferred: €10,000–€50,000/year for mid-market]
**Typical buyer:** Finance Manager, CFO at 200–2,000-employee companies

**Their strengths:**
- Broad ERP ecosystem (80+ integrations)
- Established European presence, particularly France and Spain
- Pre-built supplier network reduces onboarding friction

**Their weaknesses:**
- AP-only — no unified AR workflow
- Generic document processing, not proprietary AI
- UX can feel complex for smaller finance teams

**Why customers switch to us:**
- Dost handles AP + AR in one platform; Yooz requires separate tools
- Faster time-to-value; less configuration required on Dost's proprietary AI vs. Yooz's template setup

**How to handle "Yooz objection":**
> "Yooz is solid for AP. The question is whether you also need AR in the same flow — and whether you want to configure document templates or have AI that learns your supplier formats automatically. Most teams we talk to spend weeks in Yooz setup; Dost is live in days."

**Signal that an account uses them:**
- LinkedIn: job postings mentioning "Yooz" in requirements
- Glassdoor reviews from finance staff referencing Yooz
- Clay: technographic enrichment if available

**Competitive message for Tier 1 outreach:**
> "A few finance teams we've spoken with in [industry] came to us after outgrowing their AP tool — specifically because they needed AR in the same workflow and couldn't get their supplier formats extracted accurately without manual cleanup."

## Rossum

**One-line summary:** AI document intelligence platform for enterprise invoice and document data extraction — developer-configured, not finance-team-native.
**Market position:** Challenger (stronger in enterprise, developer-oriented)
**Pricing:** [inferred: enterprise, €30,000+/year]
**Typical buyer:** IT/Engineering team first, then finance — or enterprises with internal dev resources

**Their strengths:**
- Strong AI for complex, multi-format document extraction
- Flexible API — integrates anywhere with developer effort
- Good for high-variety document types beyond invoices

**Their weaknesses:**
- Requires developer/IT involvement to configure and maintain
- Not a finance-team-native product — setup is technical
- No native AR workflow

**Why customers switch to us:**
- Dost is configured by finance teams, not IT — no developer dependency after go-live
- Unified AP + AR vs. Rossum's AP/document focus

**How to handle "Rossum objection":**
> "Rossum is excellent if you have engineering resources to maintain it. What we hear from finance teams is they want something they can own themselves — no IT ticket every time a new supplier format shows up. Dost handles that automatically."

**Signal that an account uses them:**
- Job postings mentioning "Rossum" or "intelligent document processing"
- Engineering team LinkedIn activity around document automation

- Dost handles AR + AP in one workflow

**How to handle "Medius objection":**
> "Medius makes sense at a certain scale. The teams we're talking to at [size range] find it's more complexity than they need — 4-month implementations and pricing built for 2,000-employee organizations. Dost is designed for this exact size."

**Signal that an account uses them:**

- Finance team LinkedIn skills listing Medius

## Basware

**Their weaknesses:**

---

## Win Patterns

**vs. Yooz:** We win when the account needs AR + AP unified, or when their supplier formats are non-standard enough that template-based extraction keeps failing.

**vs. Rossum:** We win when the champion is in finance (not IT) and wants to own the tool without developer dependency.

**vs. Medius:** We win at companies with 150–600 employees where Medius implementation complexity and cost are a blocker.

**vs. Manual/Excel:** We win whenever there's an internal champion (Finance Manager or CFO) who has experienced a painful month-end close and has authority to buy.

---

## Loss Patterns

**vs. Yooz:** We lose when the account is already deeply configured in Yooz and switching cost is high, or when their primary concern is breadth of pre-connected supplier network.

**vs. Medius:** We lose at 1,000+ employee companies with complex multi-entity procurement workflows — Medius has deeper workflow features at that scale.

**vs. "Do nothing":** We lose when there's no internal champion willing to drive adoption, or when the CFO sees headcount as cheaper than software.
