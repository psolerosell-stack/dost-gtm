Last updated: 2024-03-01

## Ideal Customer Profile

### Firmographics

| Dimension | Ideal Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Employee count | 100–500 | Below 100: no dedicated ops function. Above 500: buying process too complex. |
| Revenue (if known) | $5M – $50M ARR | |
| Funding stage | Series A–C | Series D+ has internal engineering resources to build instead of buy |
| Funding recency | Raised in last 24 months | Freshly funded = budget + growth pressure |
| Industry | B2B SaaS (primary), FinTech, HR Tech (secondary) | |
| Geography | North America (primary), UK/Western Europe (secondary) | |
| Business model | B2B SaaS, subscription | |

### Technographics

**Strong fit:**
- Uses Salesforce OR HubSpot as primary CRM — our core integration points
- Uses HubSpot AND Salesforce simultaneously — highest fit signal (data sync is a constant pain)
- Has Outreach or Salesloft in stack — signals a mature outbound motion that needs ops support
- No Workato or Tray.io — these are active displacement situations, not greenfield

**Weak fit:**
- No CRM in place — too early, not ready for automation
- Uses only spreadsheets for RevOps — process immaturity, longer sales cycle
- Enterprise SSO requirements without IT involvement — deployment blocker

### Organizational signals

- Has at least one dedicated RevOps, Sales Ops, or GTM Ops person
- RevOps team size: 1–5 people (overwhelmed but funded)
- VP or Director of Sales Ops / RevOps hired in last 12 months
- Actively hiring for RevOps, Sales Ops, or GTM Engineering roles

## Tier Definitions

### Tier 1 — Dream Accounts (150 accounts)

Criteria:
- Employee count 200–500
- Series B or C raised in last 18 months
- Using Salesforce + HubSpot simultaneously
- Has Head of RevOps or Director of Sales Ops on staff
- In B2B SaaS or FinTech

These are the 150 accounts where a win is a reference customer and a case study. Full multi-channel, AE-owned, researched before every touch.

### Tier 2 — High-Fit (500–800 accounts)

Criteria:
- Employee count 100–300
- Series A or B, funded in last 24 months
- Using Salesforce OR HubSpot
- RevOps function exists (any title)

Signal-triggered sequences, SDR-owned, semi-personalized.

### Tier 3 — Good-Fit (1,000–2,000 accounts)

Criteria:
- Employee count 50–200
- Any B2B SaaS funding stage
- CRM in place

Automated sequences, light personalization, template-based.

### Tier 4 — Monitor Only

Interesting companies that don't yet meet criteria. Watch for trigger events (new funding round, RevOps hire, stack changes).

## Anti-ICP

| Exclusion | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Companies under 50 employees | No dedicated RevOps function, no budget for tooling |
| Companies over 2,000 employees | Have internal engineering capacity; buying process too long for our current motion |
| Workato customers (active contract) | Migration cost and complexity too high; wrong conversation |
| Agencies and services firms | Wrong business model — ops needs are fundamentally different |
| Non-SaaS (e-commerce, media, consumer) | Different ops stack, different pain, wrong messaging |

## Qualification Framework

### Must-have (deal-breaker if absent)
1. CRM in place (Salesforce or HubSpot)
2. Dedicated RevOps or Sales Ops person
3. B2B SaaS business model

### Strong indicators (2+ = high confidence)
1. Using multiple CRMs or CRM + MAP simultaneously
2. RevOps team size 2–5 (overwhelmed but invested)
3. Raised funding in last 18 months
4. Active hiring for RevOps roles
5. Engineering team capacity constrained (can't build internal solutions)

### Red flags (2+ = deprioritize)
1. CTO-led evaluation without business sponsor
2. Primary CRM is Pipedrive (signals early-stage, price-sensitive)
3. No defined RevOps owner
4. Already shortlisted Workato in active evaluation
